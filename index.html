CPT — Current Procedural Terminology
15 terms ▼
What is CPT?CPT stands for Current Procedural Terminology. It describes medical, surgical, and diagnostic services. Maintained and published annually by the AMA (American Medical Association). First published in 1966.

E&M — Evaluation & Management
10 terms ▼
The 3 Key Components (HEM)H = History  ·  E = Examination  ·  M = Medical Decision-Making. Time can take precedence over HEM when counseling/coordination dominates the visit.
Term / ConceptMeaning & Key Detail
3 key E&M components
History · Examination · Medical Decision-Making (HEM)
Does time override HEM?
YES — when counseling/coordination of care dominates the encounter
New patient
Has NOT been seen by same specialty in past 3 years
Established patient
HAS been seen by same specialty within past 3 years
4 patient status types
New · Established · Inpatient · Outpatient
First 3 factors in coding an encounter
Status · Type of service · Place of service
Consultation
Service provided by a physician whose opinion is requested by another provider
The 3 R's of consultations
Request · Render · Report
Subsequent hospital care
Requires only 2 of 3 key components (not all 3)
This is the E&M subcategory that is an exception to the 3-component rule
Diagnostic statement
Physician's description of the main reason for a patient encounter

ICD-10-CM Quick FactsDeveloped by NCHS (National Center for Health Statistics) to replace ICD-9-CM. Replaced on October 1, 2015. Up to 7 characters. 21 chapters organized by body system or nature of disease.
Term / ConceptMeaning & Key Detail
Developed by
